Hello World!
11 July 2016

I’ve been an introvert since childhood. I find it really difficult to go and talk to people first, in person. It takes me an enormous amount of effort to go and say ‘Hi’ to someone new. I don’t go around and introduce myself. When I come face to face with people, I’m usually left with two options — A nervous smile or pulling out my phone and looking at it to avoid them.

The irony is that I’ve been selling a SaaS product for over two years now. Typically, my day revolves around making at least fifty phone calls to prospects and as many emails. I spend a few hours talking to them everyday.

However, I’m not the typical cheerful, hyper-enthusiastic, charming guy who comes to your mind when you think about a sales guy. I’m the person who does every kind of research possible about a prospect to find things I can talk to them about and build a rapport. It doesn’t come easy to me.

No conversation does.
